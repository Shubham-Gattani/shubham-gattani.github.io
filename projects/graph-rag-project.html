Graph-based RAG for Neuroscience Research

Built a knowledge graph-driven retrieval system to answer complex neuroscience questions with paragraph-level evidence and citations.

Python Neo4j Graph RAG LLMs (LLaMA-70B) NLP / NER

Situation

Traditional vector-based RAG systems work well for simple lookups but struggle with complex, multi-hop neuroscience queries. They often rely on limited context, single-source retrieval, and lack clear traceability to original research papers, making it difficult to generate reliable, evidence-backed answers in a domain where accuracy and citations are critical.

Task

Independently design and build a complete data pipeline for a Graph-based RAG system to enable multi-hop reasoning with explicit provenance and paragraph-level citations for neuroscience research queries.

Action

  • Scraped and structured neuroscience papers: Fetched XML data from PMC and organized into paragraph-level hierarchies for fine-grained processing.
  • Engineered LLM pipelines: Used prompt-based LLaMA-70B workflows for claims, species, entity, and relationship extraction at paragraph level.
  • Implemented domain-specific NER: Combined LexiFuzz with NIF ontology and fuzzy matching to balance precision and recall in specialized neuroscience contexts.
  • Constructed Neo4j knowledge graph: Modeled with Entity and Paragraph nodes, using RELATED_TO and DESCRIBES relationships for traceability.
  • Scaled with multiprocessing: Enabled parallel processing of hundreds of papers using up to 100 concurrent workers for efficient LLM inference.
  • Enabled graph-based retrieval: Queried via Cypher to identify entities, traverse relationships, and ground responses in original text with citations.

Result

  • Enabled multi-hop reasoning: For complex neuroscience queries with explicit paragraph-level evidence and PMC references.
  • Outperformed competitors: Surpassed bio-specific LLMs (Bio-Mistral, SciLitLLM) in depth, rigor, and citation quality.
  • Achieved higher evaluation scores: In zero-shot comparisons against GPT-4o, demonstrating superior accuracy and reliability.
